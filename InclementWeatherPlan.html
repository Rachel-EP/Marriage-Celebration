Inclement Weather Day Plan

In the event of inclement weather that would affect the Niagara Falls trip or possibly the downtown Oakville trip we would instead go to Square One Shopping Centre for the day. (With the exception of transportation, prices will vary based on activity and chosen restaurants).

Food and Drink

The Food District $$

The Food District at Square One in Mississauga is the perfect spot for anyone who loves to try a variety of foods in one place. With its market-style setup, you can easily explore different specialty shops, each offering their own unique flavors and treats. From freshly baked pastries to gourmet chocolates, artisan cheeses, and international dishes, there’s something new to discover at every corner. Whether you’re in the mood for a little bit of everything or want to sample something new, The Food District makes it easy to taste your way through a wide range of delicious options all under one roof. One thing Rachel says is a must try is Sweet Jesus Ice Cream, it is only available in Canada and it is the best ice cream you will ever have! To read more on the Food District Website click here.

The Food Court $

In addition to the Food District there is also a more traditional food court with many options including New York Fries which is where Rachel gets her favourite poutine if you want to give it a try (The poutine is also vegetarian, using a mushroom gravy)! The food court in Square One is large and includes all types of food you can think of.

Surrounding Sit Down Restaurants $$$

One of the charms of the Square One Mall is it's location in the middle of the city centre surrounded by well known sit down resrtaurants. These restaurants tend to be much more high end (and therefore higher priced) than those within the mall and the food court but many are defintiely worth it. One of these restaurants that Rachel and Steven love for a date night is called Jack Astors (yes that's the real name), which offers great food for a higher but still decent price as well as having a fun atmosphere. Another option nearby is called The Keg, this is a steakhouse chain in Canada, it is very expensive but defnitiely worth the try if you love steak and/or seafood. To look at restaurants nearby the mall, click here.

Shopping and Store Directory

Most of the stores in Square One are going to be ones you have in the USA as well with a few exceptions: Roots and Simons mainly. Roots is a leather company mostly, they make purses, wallets etc. However their iconic clothing is also well known to Canadians as super comfortable and instantly recognizable; they have sweaters, sweatpants, and other workout attire. Simons is a brand that started in Montreal Quebec and is a department store for clothing and home items. While their products are very pretty and well made it can also be expensive. While looking through the directory or wandering through the mall you are also likely to find many sotres you've not seen before so feel free to check them all out! The REC Room

The REC Room would be where we may spend most of our time during this day trip to Square One as it had so many acitvities including:

  • Bowling
  • Axe throwing
  • Ping pong
  • Pool tables
  • Shuffleboard
  • Archery
  • Arcade and Video games

The REC Room also has food you can order while inside as well as a bar to order drinks (both alcoholic and non-alcoholic)

Check out more on the REC Room website.

Transportation

General transport will be through a bus that runs directly from the Oakville GO Station to Square One bus terminal (GO bus 56). This is the information on Google Maps. It is about a 50 minute bus ride. The cost each way would be about $7 CAD making the total $14 CAD. You can buy E-tickets for the bus online beforehand or you can tap your credit/debit card while getting on the bus.

If you would like to go using a ride share service like Uber it would be about a 25 minute ride and cost about $30 CAD one way. This link is to Uber's website with the location information already inputted.
