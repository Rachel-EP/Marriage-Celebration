Toronto Day Trip

Saturday, July 5th, 2025

Toronto is a dynamic and multicultural city, offering a rich blend of modernity and history. Known for its impressive skyline, anchored by the iconic CN Tower.

The city’s waterfront along Lake Ontario is a major draw, featuring scenic parks, beaches, and the lively Harbourfront Centre. For art and culture enthusiasts, the Royal Ontario Museum and the Art Gallery of Ontario offer world-class exhibits, while sports fans can catch a game at the Rogers Centre or Scotiabank Arena.

Toronto is also celebrated for its food scene, with a staggering variety of restaurants reflecting the city’s cultural diversity. From gourmet dining to street food, there's something for every palate.

Suggested Activities

The CN Tower

The CN Tower is one of Toronto’s most iconic landmarks and a must-see for any visitor to the city. Standing at a towering 553.3 meters (1,815 feet), it offers breathtaking views of Toronto and beyond, making it one of the tallest freestanding structures in the world.

Attractions and Activities at the CN Tower

  1. Observation Levels
    • Main Observation Level: At 346 meters (1,136 feet), this level offers panoramic views of the city, Lake Ontario, and, on clear days, the distance outline of Niagara Falls and even the USA. The floor-to-ceiling windows provide an uninterrupted view, making it the perfect spot for photos.
    • Glass Floor: A thrilling experience where you can walk on (or lay on) glass pannels 342meters above the ground, giving you a dizzyiing view straight down to the street below.
    • The Top: For those seeking the ultimate view, the Top, located at 447 meters (1,465 feet), is one of the highest observation platforms in the world. It provides an unparalleled vantage point.
  2. EdgeWalk: For adrenaline junkies, the CN Tower offers EdgeWalk, the world's highest full circle, hands-free walk on a 1.5 meter-wide ledge encircling the top of the Tower's main pod. At 356 meters, you'll be securely harnessed as you lean out over the edge for a heart-pounding experience.
  3. 360 Restuarant: this will be meantioned more in detail in the restaurants section but this is a unique experience at the CN Tower, a restaurant that rotates 360 degrees every 72 minutes. NOTE: If dining at 360 you do not need to purchase separate admission to the Tower, it is included in your reservation. Reservations must be made in advance.
  4. Virtual Reality Experience: For a fun and interactive addition, the CN Tower offers a state-of-the-art virtual reality experience where you can explore Toronto from new perspectives and even simulate the EdgeWalk without leaving the ground.

Booking Tickets and Arriving at the CN Tower

There are two ways to book tickets for the CN Tower: online and at the box office. The easiest way to book your tickets is online through the official CN Tower website (using this link). The box office for the CN Tower is located at the base of the tower and tickets can also be purchased directly there but avilability may be limited on the day of the visit; especially since it will be the summer. For timing, we highly recommend that if visiting the CN Tower that it is the first thing you do when we arrive as the line can get very long as the day goes on.

Ticket Options

  • General Admission ($45): Includes access to the Main Observation Level, GlassFloor, and the Outdoor SkyTerrace. Price fluctuates to $47 if bought day of.
  • The Top Ticket ($56): Add on to your general admission for access to the Top.
  • EdgeWalk Ticket ($199-$225): This includes the walk, a certificate of achievement, a video of your experience, two printed photos of your walk, and a ticket to visit the rest of the Tower (including The Top).
  • Priority ($95): Add on to a ticket, jump to the front of the line and get express elevation up to the main observation level (in a private elevator), the next available time slot to visit The Top, 15% off all purchases in the gift shop and priority elevation down. Usual wait times at the base of the Tower vary but generally you can expect to wait about an hour or even two in queue before going up making this worth thinking about.
  • Sea the Sky ($79): This option is great if you are looking to both go up the CN Tower and go across the street to Ripley's Aquarium. This ticket includes both general admission to the CN Tower and to Ripley's. NOTE: These tickets are sold online only.

The CN Tower is easily accessible from the train station, it is about a 5 minute walk.

Ripley's Aquarium

Ripley's Aquarium of Canada is right at the base of the CN Tower and is a world class attraction. With over 16,000 aquatic animals this aquarium offers an immersive expereince that's perfect for visitors of all ages.

Exhibits and Animals of Interest

  1. Dangerous Lagoon: One of the most thrilling exhibits, the Dangerous Lagoon features a moving walkway that takes you through an underwater tunnel surrounded by sharks, sea turtles, and giant singrays. This 2.5-milliom-liter habitat provides an up-close encounter with some of the ocean's most fearsome predators, including the sand tiger shark and the green sawfish.
  2. Rainbow Reef: Step into a kaleidoscope of colours in the Rainbow Reef exhibit, home to vibrant coral and over 100 species of tropical fish. Watch as angelfish, butterflyfish, and clownfish dart through the coral formations, creating a mesmerizing underwater spectacle.
  3. Canadian Waters: Celebrate the diversity of Canada's aquatic life in this exhibit, which showcases animals from across the country's lakes, rivers, and oceans. From the Great Lakes to the Pacific Kelp Forest, you'll encounter creatures like the giant Pacific octopus, American lobster, and the iconic Atlantic salmon.
  4. Planet Jellies: Enter a glowing world of jellyfish in Planet Jellies, where these ethereal creatures are displayed in colour-changing tanks. The exhibit features several species of jellies, including the moon jelly, with their delicate forms and hypnotic movements offering a serene and captivating experience.
  5. Ray Bay: Get up close and personal with some of the ocean's most graceful creatures in Ray Bay, where you'll find cownose rays, spotted eagle rays, and southern singrays gliding through the water. Interactive touch tanks also allow visitors to gently touch these animals under the guidance of trained staff.
  6. Shoreline Gallery: Before you leave, stop by the Shoreline Gallery, where you can touch and learn about the creatures of the intertidal zone, including starfish, sea urchins, and other invertebrates.

Booking Tickets

Harbourfront Centre

Harbourfront Centre is a vibrant cultural and recreational destination located on Toronto’s scenic waterfront. This 10-acre site offers a diverse range of activities and events that make it a must-visit for both locals and tourists. Overlooking Lake Ontario, the Harbourfront Centre combines arts, culture, and outdoor fun in a beautiful setting that’s perfect for exploration year-round.

Activities and Attractions at Harbourfront Centre

  1. Art Galleries and Exhibitions:
    • The Power Plant Contemporary Art Gallery: One of Canada’s leading contemporary art galleries, The Power Plant showcases cutting-edge art from local and international artists. Admission is free, making it a great place to experience modern art in an inspiring environment.
    • Artport Gallery: Located within the Harbourfront Centre, the Artport Gallery offers rotating exhibitions that highlight emerging and established artists in various media, including photography, sculpture, and painting.
  2. Recreational Activities: At the Harbourfront Centre you can participate in several recreational activities. During the warm months, visitors can rent paddleboats, kayaks, or canoes to explore the Toronto Islands or simply enjoy a relaxing paddle on the lake. You can also rent bikes and enjoy the walking trails. The Martin Goodman Trail runs through Harbourfront Centre, providing a picturesque path for walking, jogging, or cycling along the waterfront.
  3. Shopping and Dining: For shopping you can explore the Harbourfront Centre Craft and Design Studios where you can watch artists at work and purchase unique, handmade items ranging from ceramics to textiles and jewelry. For dining you can enjoy lunch from one of the several waterfront restaurants and cafes where you can enjoy a meal or a drink with a view of the lake.

Casa Loma

Casa Loma, Toronto’s majestic castle, offers a unique journey back in time, especially enchanting during the summer months. This Gothic Revival masterpiece, perched on a hill overlooking the city, was built in the early 20th century by financier Sir Henry Pellatt. With its grand architecture, lush gardens, and sweeping views of Toronto, Casa Loma is a must-visit for history buffs, architecture enthusiasts, and anyone seeking a fairy-tale experience.

Attractions at Casa Loma

  • Grand Hall and Library: Step into the opulence of the Grand Hall with its 60 ft high seiling and stunning chandeliers. The adjacent library, with its intricate woodwork and rich decor, is a highlight of the castle's interior.
  • The Towers: Climb the winding staircases to the top of the castle's towers for breathtaking panoramic views of Toronto's skyline and Lake Ontario.
  • Secret Passages: Discover hidden doors and secret passages as you explore the various rooms and corridors of Casa Loma, adding a touch of mystery to your visit.
  • Beautiful Gardens: During the summer, Casa Loma's five acres of gardens are in full bloom providing a picturesque setting for a leisurely stroll. The gardens feature a variety of flowers, fountains, and sculptures. You can also enjoy a light meal or refreshing drink on the Garden Terrace which offers lovely views of the castle and the surrounding gardens.
  • Escape Rooms: For those looking for adventure, Casa Loma offers themed escape rooms within the castle's historic rooms. These immersive experiences challenge you to solve puzles and unravel mysteries in settings like the "Kingdom Adventure" or "Station M."
  • Thematic Exhibits: Throughout the year, Casa Loma hosts rotating exhibits related to its history or featuring specific themes such as vintage cars, historical artifacts, and art installations.

Tickets and Visiting Fees

General admission for adults is typically priced around $45 CAD Escape room tickets are separate tickets from general admission. Both of which can be booked online. Prices for the escape rooms vary based on the theme and timing but typically range from $40 to $50 CAD per person. To book tickets beforehand or to check pricing use this link.

Getting to Casa Loma

To get to Casa Loma you could take a taxi/Uber or take the number 1 bus from Union Station to Dupont Station (about a 10 minute ride) and then a 10 minute walk to Casa Loma.

Art Gallery of Ontario

The Art Gallery of Ontario (AGO) is one of the largest and most distinguished art museums in North America, offering an inspiring exploration of art from around the world and across centuries. Located in the heart of downtown Toronto, the AGO is renowned for its expansive collection, innovative exhibitions, and stunning architectural design by Frank Gehry.

Permanent Collections at the Museum

Canadian Art: The AGO is home to an extensive collection of Canadian art, including works by the renowned Group of Seven, who are celebrated for their iconic landscapes. The collection also features pieces by Indigenous artists, showcasing the rich cultural heritage of Canada’s First Nations, Inuit, and Métis communities.

European Art: The European collection spans from the Renaissance to the 20th century, featuring masterpieces by artists like Rembrandt, Rubens, and Monet. Highlights include the AGO’s impressive collection of Baroque and Impressionist works.

Contemporary Art: The AGO’s contemporary collection includes works by leading international and Canadian artists. The gallery regularly hosts cutting-edge exhibitions that explore current themes in art, society, and culture.

Indigenous Art: In addition to its Canadian Indigenous collection, the AGO is committed to showcasing global Indigenous art, offering visitors the opportunity to engage with diverse perspectives and traditions.

Special Exhibitions

The AGO hosts rotating special exhibitions that highlight both historical and contemporary art. These exhibitions often feature works on loan from other major museums and private collections, providing unique opportunities to see rare and significant pieces.

Architecture and Design

The AGO’s building itself is a work of art. Redesigned by the acclaimed architect Frank Gehry, the gallery features a striking blend of glass, wood, and steel, creating a light-filled space that harmonizes with the art it houses. The iconic Galleria Italia, a sweeping wood-and-glass walkway, offers stunning views of the city and is a highlight of any visit.

Tickets and Visiting Information

General admission for adults is typically priced around $25 CAD, providing access to the gallery's permanent collections and most special exhibitions.

Getting to the AGO

To get to the AGO you can either take a taxi/Uber or take bus number 1 from Union Station to St. Patrick Station (about a 4 minute bus ride) and then a 6 minute walk from the bus station to the AGO.

Royal Ontario Museum

The Royal Ontario Museum (ROM) is one of the largest and most comprehensive museums in North America, offering a fascinating journey through natural history, world cultures, and art. Located at the intersection of Bloor Street and Avenue Road in downtown Toronto, the ROM’s iconic Michael Lee-Chin Crystal entrance sets the stage for an experience that blends the ancient with the modern. Whether you’re interested in dinosaurs, ancient civilizations, or contemporary design, the ROM’s diverse collections and engaging exhibits make it a must-visit destination.

Highlights of the ROM

  1. Dinosaur and Natural History:
    • Fossils and Dinosaurs: The ROM is home to an impressive collection of dinosaur fossils, including the towering Tyrannosaurus rex, Triceratops, and Stegosaurus. The James and Louise Temerty Galleries of the Age of Dinosaurs take you back millions of years to explore the prehistoric world.
    • Gems and Minerals: Explore the Earth's treasures in the Teck Suite of Galleries: Earth's Treasures, featuring a stunning array of gems, minerals, and meteorites. Don't miss the famous Light of the Desert, one of the largest cut cerussite gemstones in the world.
    • Biodiversity: The ROM’s Life in Crisis: Schad Gallery of Biodiversity highlights the incredible diversity of life on Earth and the challenges facing our planet’s ecosystems. The gallery features specimens from all over the world, including endangered species and rare animals.
  2. World Cultures:
    • Ancient Egypt: Discover the wonders of Ancient Egypt in the Galleries of Africa: Egypt, where you’ll find mummies, sarcophagi, and artifacts that tell the story of this ancient civilization. The ROM’s Egyptian collection is one of the finest in North America.
    • Chinese Temple Art: The ROM’s Gallery of Chinese Architecture is a unique space that features large-scale temple art, including monumental sculptures and murals from China’s Buddhist heritage. This gallery offers a rare glimpse into the religious and artistic traditions of ancient China.
    • Indigenous Cultures: The Daphne Cockwell Gallery of Canada: First Peoples is dedicated to the art, history, and culture of Canada’s Indigenous peoples. The gallery features artifacts, textiles, and art that celebrate the rich cultural heritage of the First Nations, Inuit, and Métis.
  3. Art and Design
    • European Art: The Samuel European Galleries showcase a wide range of European art and design from the Middle Ages to the 20th century. The collection includes paintings, sculptures, and decorative arts from renowned artists and artisans.
    • Textiles and Fashion: The ROM’s Patricia Harris Gallery of Textiles & Costume offers an exquisite collection of textiles, garments, and accessories from around the world. The gallery frequently hosts special exhibitions that explore fashion history and design.
    • Modern and Contemporary Art: The ROM also features rotating exhibitions of modern and contemporary art, highlighting works by both Canadian and international artists.
  4. Special Exhibitions The ROM regularly hosts special exhibitions that cover a wide range of topics, from ancient civilizations to cutting-edge contemporary art. These exhibitions often feature rare artifacts on loan from other museums and private collections.

Tickets and Visiting Information

General admission for adults typically costs around $23 CAD, granting access to the museum's permanent galleries and most exhibitions. Some special exhibitions may require an ticket or an upgrade from the general admission. Prices for these special exhibitions vary but they often range from $10 to $15 CAD extra.

Getting to the ROM

To get to the ROM you can either take a taxi/Uber or take bus number 1 from Union Station to Museum Station (about an 8 minute bus ride) and then a 1 minute walk from the bus station to the ROM.

Kensington Market

Kensington Market is one of Toronto’s most eclectic and vibrant neighborhoods, known for its bohemian atmosphere, diverse culture, and unique blend of shops, restaurants, and art. Located just west of downtown, this historic market district is a melting pot of cultures, where you can experience the city’s rich multicultural heritage through food, fashion, and art. Whether you're a foodie, a vintage shopper, or simply looking to soak in the local vibe, Kensington Market offers a sensory feast that reflects the spirit of Toronto.

Highlights of Kensington Market

  1. Global Cuisine Kensington Market is a paradise for food lovers, offering a wide range of international cuisines. You can savor dishes from all corners of the world, from authentic Mexican tacos and Jamaican patties to Ethiopian injera and Portuguese pastries.
  2. Vintage and Independent Shopping
    • Vintage Clothing Stores: Kensinton Market is famoud for its vintage shops, where you can hunt for one-of-a-kind fashion pieces, retro accessories, and timeless garments. Stores like Exile and Flashback offer a treasure trove of vintage finds from different eras.
    • Independent Boutiques: Beyond vintage, Kensington is full of independent boutiques selling handmade jewelry, local art, and quirky home décor. These shops are perfect for finding unique gifts and supporting local artisans.
  3. Street Art and Murals: The streets of Kensington Market are a canvas for Toronto's vibrant street art scene. Colorful murals and graffiti adorn the walls, adding to the area’s creative and laid-back atmosphere. Walking through the market, you’ll encounter art that reflects the neighborhood’s history, diversity, and artistic spirit.
  4. Cafes and Chill Spots: Kensinton Market is dotted with cozy cafes and coffee shops, perfect for taking a break and people-watching. Whether you're in the mood for a strong espresso, a fresh smoothie, or a craft beer, you'll find plenty of spots fto relax and soak in the market's ambiance.

Getting to Kensington Market

You can get to Kensington Market multiple ways, walking there from Union Station takes about 39 minutes, taking public transit will take about 30 minutes. For public transit you'd either walk up to Dundas station (21 minutes) or take bus number 1 from Union up to Dundas station. From Dundas station you take the 505 streetcar for about 13 minutes to get off at Dundas St West at Denison Ave. From that street car station it is about a 5 minute walk to Kensington Market.
